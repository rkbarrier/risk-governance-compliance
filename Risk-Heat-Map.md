# Enterprise Risk Heat Map

## Executive Overview

An Enterprise Risk Heat Map provides leadership with a visual representation of organizational risk by evaluating the likelihood and business impact of identified risks. It helps governance committees prioritize mitigation efforts, allocate resources effectively, and monitor the organization's overall risk profile.

> **Portfolio Note:** This document is an original example created for professional portfolio purposes. It is inspired by enterprise banking governance and operational risk practices and does not contain confidential or proprietary employer information.

---

# Purpose

The purpose of this Enterprise Risk Heat Map is to:

* Prioritize enterprise risks.
* Support executive decision-making.
* Focus mitigation efforts on high-impact risks.
* Improve governance reporting.
* Monitor changes in organizational risk over time.

---

# Risk Rating Methodology

## Likelihood

| Rating | Description                          |
| ------ | ------------------------------------ |
| Low    | Unlikely to occur                    |
| Medium | May occur under certain conditions   |
| High   | Expected to occur without mitigation |

## Business Impact

| Rating | Description                                               |
| ------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Low    | Minimal operational disruption                            |
| Medium | Noticeable business impact requiring management attention |
| High   | Significant financial, regulatory, or customer impact     |

# Executive Risk Priorities

## High Priority

* Payment Processing Availability
* Third-Party Service Disruption
* Cybersecurity Threats

### Executive Actions

* Increase resiliency testing.
* Enhance vendor oversight.
* Expand cybersecurity monitoring.
* Review executive recovery reporting.

---

## Medium Priority

* Data Quality
* Regulatory Change
* Capacity Planning

### Executive Actions

* Strengthen validation controls.
* Monitor regulatory developments.
* Review technology capacity forecasts.

---

## Low Priority

* Minor System Defects
* Reporting Delays
* Manual Processing Errors

### Executive Actions

* Continue operational monitoring.
* Review trends during governance meetings.
* Address through continuous improvement initiatives.
